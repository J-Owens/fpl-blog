Overview

A dominant shooting display from West Ham, racking up an incredible 3.5 npxG, which was converted to an even higher 3.7 PSxG.

Judging by the NSxG, Crystal Palace had the better of the possession-based danger but failed to turn that into shots on goal. This is further backed up by Palace making 38 passes into the attacking third to West Hams 28 and making 14 passes into the oppositions penalty area to West Hams 5. However, despite having more possession (53% to 47%), as many touches in the attacking third (170 v 169), and as many touches in the attacking penalty area (25 vs 26) they could not convert this possession into shots, as Palace only managed to pull off 6 shots to West Hams 17. West Ham were direct and made great decisions on the day regarding when to shoot.

In terms of FPL assets, Antonio owners have Guaita to blame for him only walking away with an assist from a match where he managed to pull off 1.6 NPxG, which is an incredibly high number.

Soucek, a darling of the analytic Twitter bubble, scored 2 of West Hams goals, and despite fume on Twitter, he is not outperforming his xG by an unprecedented amount (1.8), and his underlying numbers are marginally better than they were last season. He was a good pick pre-season, he has been a good pick all season, and he continues to be a good pick. But his returns will be sporadic and hard to predict. As always, he will be more likely to return against teams that are worse in defence, but further than that he may be more likely to return against teams that we can identify who are poor at dealing with set pieces. I own him and have for the entire season. He will probably rarely get into my 11 and it hurts to see him haul on the bench, but it is what it is, you play the odds and most weeks you will have better players to put out. One thing to consider is squad structure. Having Soucek as your 8th attacker vs having him as your 7th attacker (and therefore always started assuming you always play a 3-4-3 or 3-5-2). Temper your expectations depending on this and consider what you could get with the money instead if your 8th attacker was an ultra-budget player.

From my point of view as a non-West Ham defence owner it was nice to see them concede, although fantastic finishing from Palace who converted 0.5 xG to 1.4 PSxG is the reason for this West Ham probably defended well to limit Palace to 0.5 xG or alternatively it could have been purely bad decision making on Palaces behalf.

FPL Players to watch:

Crystal Palace:

Guaita A good value goalkeeper with some good upcoming fixtures.

Mitchell If he continues starting, a 3.9 starting defender is always enticing. I have seen suggestions online that Mitchell providers the defensive cover required that Hodgson wasnt getting from PVA.

Zaha The highest ceiling CP asset at a good price.

Overall, I would steer clear of Palace players as they have no extra fixtures to fit in and we should be trying to organise our teams to prepare for the upcoming double gameweeks and blank gameweeks as best we can with our free transfers.

West Ham:

Antonio A budget priced attacker with elite numbers since the beginning of last season, with no competition for his place in the starting line-up. If not for West Ham having no doubles, he would be an absolute must own. It depends how the DGWs and BGWs line up as to when I sell and buy him back.

Soucek An S tier value pick. Capable as being a rotational/bench upgrade 8th attacker or a fully fledged 4th attacker to give you money to invest in your other players.

Other than these value monsters I would steer clear of other West Ham assets. They do not have extra fixtures to squeeze in and they do not have the best run of defensive fixtures.
